"1984" by George Orwell is a dystopian novel that explores the dangers of totalitarianism and extreme political ideology. Published in 1949, the story is set in a grim future where the world is ruled by three totalitarian superstates. The novel follows the life of Winston Smith, a low-ranking member of the ruling Party in London, in the nation of Oceania. The Party, led by the enigmatic Big Brother, employs omnipresent surveillance and mind control to maintain its power and suppress any form of dissent.
