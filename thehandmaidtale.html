The Handmaid’s Tale

The Handmaid's Tale by Margaret Atwood is a dystopian novel first published in 1985. The story is set in a near-future society called Gilead, which has replaced the United States. In this theocratic and totalitarian regime, women's rights are severely restricted, and they are classified by their roles in society.
