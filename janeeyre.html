Jane Eyre

Charlotte Brontë

★★★★☆

Jane Eyre is a coming-of-age story about an orphaned girl who grows up to become an independent and strong-willed woman. The novel follows the life of Jane Eyre, from her difficult childhood under the care of her cruel aunt and harsh boarding school, to her time as a governess at Thornfield Hall. There, she meets the enigmatic and brooding Mr. Rochester, who harbors dark secrets. The novel explores themes of love, morality, social class, and gender equality. As Jane's relationship with Mr. Rochester develops, she must confront her own values, self-respect, and the deep moral dilemmas arising from her love for him. The novel is famous for its Gothic atmosphere, psychological depth, and portrayal of a strong, self-respecting woman who defies social expectations.
