The Kite Runner is a novel by Khaled Hosseini that was first published in 2003. The story is set in Afghanistan and spans from the late 1970s to the early 2000s. It follows the life of Amir, a young boy from a wealthy family, and his complicated friendship with Hassan, the son of his father's servant. The novel explores themes of guilt, redemption, and the personal cost of betrayal.
