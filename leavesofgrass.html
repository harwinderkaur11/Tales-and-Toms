Leaves of Grass is a renowned collection of poetry by Walt Whitman, first published in 1855. Whitman continued to revise and expand the collection throughout his life, with the final edition, known as the "Deathbed Edition," published in 1892. The poems in Leaves of Grass celebrate themes such as nature, democracy, individualism, and the human experience.
